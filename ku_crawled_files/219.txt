                    <h3>Notable Alumni<a class="pull-right kualumni" href="http://www.kualumni.org/"><small><em>KU Alumni Association &raquo;</em></small></a></h3>
<hr>
<h5>K.S. "Boots" Adams <span>(1899&ndash;1975)</span></h5>
<p>Former president and CEO of Phillips 66 Petroleum Co. Adams Alumni Center, of which he was a major donor, is named for him</p>
<hr>
<h5>Forrest C. "Phog" Allen <span>(1885&ndash;1974)</span></h5>
<p>"Father of Basketball Coaching," KU coach (1919&ndash;56, national championships 1921, 1922, 1952); creator of NCAA basketball tournament. Coached by James Naismith at KU</p>
<hr>
<h5>Phillip Anschutz</h5>
<p>Founder, Qwest Communications, a multimedia corporation, film company Walden Media (<em>Holes</em>, <em>Because of Winn-Dixie</em>, the <em>Chronicles of Narnia</em> series)</p>
<hr>
<h5>Harold Arlin <span>(1896&ndash;1986)</span></h5>
<p>Radio pioneer who broadcast the first scheduled program, the results of the 1920 presidential election; first broadcaster to call a baseball game and a college football game, 1921</p>
<hr>
<h5>Sheila Bair</h5>
<p>Chairwoman, Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. (2006&ndash;11). Recipient, Honorary Degree, 2012</p>
<hr>
<h5>Scott Bakula</h5>
<p>Actor (<em>Quantum Leap</em>, <em>Star Trek: Enterprise</em>)</p>
<hr>
<h5>Etta Moten Barnett <span>(1901&ndash;2004)</span></h5>
<p>Actress and singer, first African-American woman to entertain at the White House</p>
<hr>
<h5>Mark Batenic</h5>
<p>CEO, IGA, Inc., largest independent grocers affiliation in world with 4,000 stores in 41 nations</p>
<hr>
<h5>Carol Beier</h5>
<p>Justice, Kansas Supreme Court (appointed 2003)</p>
<hr>
<h5>Robert F. Bennett <span>(1927&ndash;2000)</span></h5>
<p>39th governor of Kansas (1975&ndash;79)</p>
<hr>
<h5>David G. Booth</h5>
<p>Co-founder of Dimensional Fund Advisors; owner of James Naismith's original 13 rules to basketball</p>
<hr>
<h5>William E. Borah <span>(1865&ndash;1940)</span></h5>
<p>Attorney and influential U.S. senator from Idaho (1906&ndash;40); 1889 graduate; sponsored bills establishing labor department, Children's Bureau</p>
<hr>
<h5>George L. Brown <span>(1926&ndash;2006)</span></h5>
<p>First African-American elected lieutenant governor in the U.S. (1974), and first African-American elected to statewide office in Colorado</p>
<hr>
<h5>Peter Brown</h5>
<p>Former president, CEO and chairman of AMC Entertainment Inc., North America's second-largest theater chain</p>
<hr>
<h5>Sam Brownback</h5>
<p>46th Governor of Kansas, 2011&ndash;; former member of U.S. Senate and U.S. House of Representatives</p>
<hr>
<h5>Anand Burman</h5>
<p>Chairman, Dabur India, a $3.1 billion consumer goods company specializing in pharmaceuticals</p>
<hr>
<h5>Francisco Santos Calderón</h5>
<p>Former vice president, Republic of Colombia (2002&ndash;10)</p>
<hr>
<h5><a href="http://www.news.ku.edu/2008/november/7/carroll.shtml">Cynthia Carroll</a></h5>
<p>CEO of Anglo-American, one of the world's largest mining conglomerates</p>
<hr>
<h5>Wilt Chamberlain <span>(1936&ndash;99)</span></h5>
<p> NBA superstar (1959&ndash;73), Philadelphia/San Francisco Warriors, Philadelphia 76ers, Los Angeles Lakers</p>
<hr>
<h5>John E. Christensen</h5>
<p>Chancellor, University of Nebraska-Omaha</p>
<hr>
<h5>Evan S. Connell <span>(1924&ndash;2013)</span></h5>
<p>Novelist and poet (<em>Mrs. Bridge</em>, <em>Mr. Bridge</em>, <em>Son of the Morning Star: Custer and the Little Bighorn</em>)</p>
<hr>
<h5>Linda Zarda Cook</h5>
<p>Former executive director of Shell Gas &amp; Power and CEO of Shell Canada</p>
<hr>
<h5>Dale R. Corson <span>(1914&ndash;2012)</span></h5>
<p>President, Cornell University (1969&ndash;1977), physicist who discovered basic element astatine (AT)</p>
<hr>
<h5>David B. Dillon </h5>
<p>Chairman and CEO, Kroger Co.</p>
<hr>
<h5>George Docking <span>(1904&ndash;64)</span></h5>
<p>35th governor of Kansas (1957&ndash;61)</p>
<hr>
<h5>Robert B. Docking <span>(1925&ndash;83)</span></h5>
<p>38th governor of Kansas (1967&ndash;75). Son of George Docking</p>
<hr>
<h5>Bob Dole</h5>
<p>Kansas senator (1966&ndash;96), U.S. Senate majority leader, presidential and vice-presidential nominee. Recipient, Honorary Degree, 2012</p>
