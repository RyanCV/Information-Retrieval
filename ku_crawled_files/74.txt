    //Use javascript to avoid weather caching
    $kuj(document).ready(function() {
        if(!$kuj.cookie('myweather')){
            var url = '//webmedia.ku.edu/~http/ssi/2012/widgets/cmslawrenceweather.php';
            var weatherval = '<div class="sectiontitle">Current Weather</div>';
            var date = new Date();
            var minutes = 30;
            date.setTime(date.getTime() + (minutes * 60 * 1000));
            try {
            //cross script issue with IE8 workaround
            if (navigator.appVersion.match(/MSIE/) && window.XDomainRequest) {
                // Use Microsoft XDR
                var xdr = new XDomainRequest();
                xdr.open("get", url);
                xdr.onload = function () {
                    var result = weatherval+xdr.responseText;
                    $kuj.cookie('myweather', result, { expires: date });
                    $kuj('#weatherholder').html(result);
                };
                xdr.send();
            } else if (!navigator.appVersion.match(/MSIE/)) {
                $kuj.ajax({
                    type: 'GET',
                    url: url,
                    success: function (data) {
                        date.setTime(date.getTime() + (minutes * 60 * 1000));
                        $kuj.cookie('myweather', weatherval+data, { expires: date });
                        $kuj('#weatherholder').html($kuj.cookie('myweather'));
                    }
                });
            }
        } catch (e) {}
        }else{
            $kuj('#weatherholder').html($kuj.cookie('myweather'));
        }

    });

<p>Off Campus Student Jobs are federally subsidized by the job location and development (JLD) program sponsored by the <a href="/home">University Career Center</a> (UCC) and the <a href="http://affordability.ku.edu/">Office of Financial Aid &amp; Scholarships</a> that locates and develops off-campus job opportunities in Lawrence, Topeka and the Kansas City Metropolitan Area. Our goal is to assist University of Kansas students in obtaining off-campus jobs (full or part-time, seasonal, or temporary positions) while they are attending KU, thereby enhancing student success. All off campus student jobs are posted in <a href="https://ku-csm.symplicity.com/students/index.php?s=jobs&amp;ss=jobs&amp;&amp;job_type=4&amp;_ksl=1">KU Career Connections</a>.</p>

<p><span style="color:#FF0000;"><strong>NOTICE:</strong></span> <a href="http://www2.ed.gov/programs/fws/index.html">Federal Work Study</a> is managed by the <a href="http://affordability.ku.edu/">Office of Financial Aid &amp; Scholarships</a>, <a href="mailto:financialaid@ku.edu">financialaid@ku.edu</a> / 785-864-4700.</p>

<p><strong>Student benefits:</strong></p>

<ul>
	<li>Earn extra money for college</li>
	<li>Build your resume</li>
	<li>Develop new job skills</li>
	<li>Gain valuable professional experience</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Career Advancement Program for student employees</strong></p>

<p>If you are interested in learning more about how to best utilize the experiences and skills you have gained from your part-time job, you should consider completing the version of the Career Advancement Program that focuses on applying your part-time position to your future career goals. Email <a href="mailto:cap@ku.edu">cap@ku.edu</a>&nbsp;expressing your interest&nbsp;to start the self-directed and self-paced program, which is managed through Blackboard.</p>

<p><strong>Employer benefits:</strong></p>

<ul>
	<li>Students represent a variety of majors with diverse skills and experiences</li>
	<li>The process of filling job openings with qualified applicants becomes easier</li>
</ul>

<p><span style="color:#FF0000;"><strong>NOTICE:</strong></span> <a href="http://www2.ed.gov/programs/fws/index.html">Federal Work Study</a> is managed by the <a href="http://affordability.ku.edu/">Office of Financial Aid &amp; Scholarships</a>, <a href="mailto:financialaid@ku.edu">financialaid@ku.edu</a> / 785-864-4700.</p>
The Kansas Work-Study Program (Career Component) is funded by the State of Kansas, through the Board of Regents. The purpose of the program is to help Kansas employers hire university students to work in career-related positions. The student benefits by gaining work experience that enhances the student’s academic program. The employer benefits by being reimbursed for one half of the student’s gross wages.

<div class="accordion">For students</div>

<div class="accordion_content">In order to participate in the K.W.S. program, the student must meet several eligibility requirements. The student must:
<ul>
	<li>be a Kansas resident, paying in-state tuition fees</li>
	<li>be enrolled in at least 6 credit hours during the Fall and Spring</li>
	<li>have a cumulative G.P.A. of 2.0 or greater and must maintain a 2.0 or higher throughout the semester</li>
	<li>have a declared major in a field that is directly related to the type of employment opportunity for which the student is applying</li>
	<li>demonstrate an “established financial need” which has been defined as the cost of attendance minus any scholarship or fellowship monies awarded to the eligible student and without any financial aid received by the eligible student pursuant to title IV of the higher education act of 1965, as amended. This will be determined by the Office of Student Financial Aid.</li>
</ul>

<p><a href="http://ku-csm.symplicity.com/surveys/kwsstudent">Student Application</a></p>
In order for employers and students to become participants in the K.W.S. program, several things have to happen.

<ul>
	<li>The employer must complete an <a href="http://ku-csm.symplicity.com/surveys/kwsemployer">online application</a> providing information about the organization and the types of students who will be employed.</li>
	<li>The student must complete an <a href="http://ku-csm.symplicity.com/surveys/kwsstudent">online application</a> providing appropriate related information. The students’ application is checked against the university student records database to verify resident status, hours of enrollment, major, and G.P.A. The Office of Student Financial Aid then determines the financial eligibility of the student applicant.</li>
	<li>When an employer is deemed to be eligible and when there is at least one eligible student who will be employed by that employer, then a contract is issued to the employer.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="accordion">For employers</div>

<div class="accordion_content">To participate in the Kansas Career Work-Study Program, employers must meet the following criteria:
<ul>
	<li>Have a FEIN (Federal Employee Identification Number)</li>
	<li>Be a Kansas business using a Kansas bank</li>
	<li>Be willing to act as the student's employer (students are not employed by our office)</li>
</ul>

<p>Employers are responsible for interviewing and hiring eligible students.&nbsp; Before signing a KCWS contract, the employer must employ or be in the process of hiring an eligible student.&nbsp; Employers may advertise part-time positions for students in <a href="/employers">KU Career Connections</a>.<br />
<br />
The amount of the contract is based on the student's hourly wage, the estimated hours per week that the student will work, and the number of weeks that the student will work; Fifty percent of this amount will be reimbursed to the employer; If the estimate is too low, an amendment may be granted if funds are available.<br />
<br />
Employers must submit invoices to KCWS at least once a month.&nbsp; It can take up to four weeks for the reimbursement check to arrive - it is to the employer's benefit to submit invoices in a timely manner</p>

<p><a href="/sites/career.drupal.ku.edu/files/files/jobsandinternships/KCWS%20employer%20info.pdf">KCWS (pdf)</a> Employer Guidelines</p>

<p><a href="http://ku-csm.symplicity.com/surveys/kwsemployer">KCWS Employer Application</a></p>

<p>If you have further questions about Kansas Career Work-Study, please contact us at (785) 864-7664 or <a href="mailto:kws@ku.edu">kswkstd@ku.edu</a>.</p>
</div>
